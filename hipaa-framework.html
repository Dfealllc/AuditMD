Foundation

What HIPAA Requires of Health IT Systems

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) establishes the national standard for protecting sensitive patient health information. For organizations deploying AI-powered clinical tools, EHR systems, and digital health platforms, HIPAA compliance is not optional — it is the operational baseline.

📋

Privacy Rule

Governs how covered entities and their business associates use and disclose Protected Health Information (PHI). Establishes patient rights around their own health records including access, correction, and restriction.

🔒

Security Rule

Requires covered entities to implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect Electronic PHI (ePHI). Specifies both required and addressable implementation specifications.

🚨

Breach Notification Rule

Mandates notification to affected individuals, HHS, and in some cases media within 60 days of discovering a breach of unsecured PHI. Requires documented breach assessment procedures.

⚖️

Enforcement Rule

Establishes civil money penalties up to $1.9M per violation category per year. The Office for Civil Rights (OCR) conducts compliance investigations and resolution agreements.

Security Rule Deep Dive

The Three Pillars of HIPAA Security Safeguards

AuditMD's assessment evaluates all 18 required and addressable implementation specifications across administrative, physical, and technical safeguard domains.

🏛️

Administrative Safeguards

9 Standards

Security Management Process

Risk analysis and risk management programs, sanction policies, and information system activity review to prevent, detect, contain, and correct security violations.

Assigned Security Responsibility

Designating a Security Officer responsible for developing and implementing security policies and procedures across the covered entity.

Workforce Security

Authorization, supervision, clearance procedures, and termination processes to ensure only authorized personnel access ePHI systems.

Information Access Management

Role-based access controls, access authorization processes, and establishment and modification of access rights for all ePHI systems.

Security Awareness & Training

Ongoing security training for all workforce members including protection against malware, monitoring login attempts, and password management procedures.

Security Incident Procedures

Documented procedures for identifying, reporting, and responding to suspected or known security incidents, including tracking outcomes.

Contingency Planning

Data backup, disaster recovery, emergency mode operation, testing, and revision plans ensuring ePHI availability during emergency situations.

Evaluation

Periodic technical and non-technical evaluation of security policies and procedures in response to environmental or operational changes.

Business Associate Contracts

Written contracts with all business associates ensuring they appropriately safeguard ePHI and comply with applicable HIPAA requirements.

🏢

Physical Safeguards

4 Standards

Facility Access Controls

Contingency operations, facility security plans, access control and validation procedures, and maintenance records for physical access to ePHI systems.

Workstation Use

Policies and procedures specifying proper functions of and physical attributes of the surroundings of workstations that access ePHI.

Workstation Security

Physical safeguards for workstations that access ePHI to restrict access to authorized users only, including screen locks and cable locks.

Device & Media Controls

Disposal of hardware and electronic media containing ePHI, media re-use procedures, accountability tracking, and data backup and storage requirements.

💻

Technical Safeguards

5 Standards

Access Controls

Unique user identification, emergency access procedures, automatic logoff, and encryption/decryption mechanisms for all systems accessing ePHI.

Audit Controls

Hardware, software, and procedural mechanisms to record and examine activity in systems containing or using ePHI — audit logs, access records, and anomaly detection.

Integrity Controls

Policies and procedures ensuring ePHI is not improperly altered or destroyed, including electronic error-checking mechanisms and data integrity validation.

Person Authentication

Procedures to verify that a person seeking access to ePHI is who they claim to be — multi-factor authentication, biometrics, and identity verification protocols.

Transmission Security

Encryption of ePHI in transit, integrity controls preventing improper modification during transmission, and secure protocols for all data exchange.

Our Methodology

How AuditMD Assesses Your HIPAA Posture

Our structured HIPAA assessment process delivers a complete compliance picture in 14 days — without disrupting clinical operations.

1

Pre-Call Questionnaire & Scoping

We gather your organization's profile: EHR systems in use, number of users with ePHI access, existing policies, cloud infrastructure, and prior audit history. This scopes the assessment to your actual risk surface before we begin.

2

Stakeholder Survey & IT Governance Review

Key stakeholders — clinical leadership, IT, compliance officers — complete structured surveys capturing policy gaps, access control practices, and incident response readiness. Our IT Governance Assessment identifies structural vulnerabilities before they become violations.

3

HIPAA Compliance Assessment (12 Domains)

Our proprietary 12-domain assessment framework maps directly to HIPAA Security Rule implementation specifications. We evaluate all required and addressable specifications, score each domain, and identify compliance gaps with documented evidence.

4

Risk Analysis & Gap Documentation

We perform the formal risk analysis HIPAA requires — identifying threats and vulnerabilities to ePHI, evaluating current controls, determining likelihood and impact, and assigning risk levels. All findings are documented in a Risk Register.

5

Executive Report & Remediation Roadmap

Delivery includes your Compliance Scorecard, Executive Summary for board presentation, Risk Register with prioritized findings, and a 90-day Remediation Roadmap with actionable steps mapped to the responsible team member or vendor.

6

Post-Audit Debrief & Ongoing Monitoring (Optional)

A structured debrief session walks through findings with your leadership team. Organizations can then enroll in Layer 2 monthly compliance monitoring for continuous HIPAA posture review, regulatory alert tracking, and an annual re-audit.

Quick Reference

Key HIPAA Implementation Specifications

Required specifications must be implemented. Addressable specifications must be implemented if reasonable and appropriate — or the covered entity must document why an equivalent alternative was adopted.

Specification Safeguard Category Status What AuditMD Evaluates
Risk Analysis Administrative Required Scope, methodology, threat/vulnerability identification, likelihood and impact ratings, existing controls
Risk Management Administrative Required Risk reduction plan, implementation timelines, accountability assignments, review cycles
Unique User Identification Technical Required Absence of shared credentials, individual account provisioning, identity verification at access points
Emergency Access Procedure Technical Required Break-glass procedures, emergency access logging, post-emergency review protocols
Audit Controls Technical Required Log retention policies, access log completeness, anomaly detection, log review cadence
Transmission Encryption Technical Addressable TLS versions in use, VPN configurations, ePHI sent via email, secure messaging platforms
Workforce Training Administrative Addressable Training frequency, content coverage, completion tracking, phishing simulation programs
Device & Media Disposal Physical Required Hardware disposition procedures, data destruction certificates, BYOD policy compliance
Business Associate Agreements Administrative Required BAA coverage gaps, contract language against HHS requirements, subcontractor BAA chain
Contingency Plan Administrative Required Data backup frequency, RTO/RPO definitions, disaster recovery test cadence, documented procedures
