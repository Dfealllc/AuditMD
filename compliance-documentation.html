HIPAA Compliance Framework — Full Detail

Security Rule Deep Dive

The Three Pillars of HIPAA Security Safeguards

AuditMD's assessment evaluates all 18 required and addressable implementation specifications across administrative, physical, and technical safeguard domains.

🏛️

Administrative Safeguards

9 Standards

Security Management Process

Risk analysis and risk management programs, sanction policies, and information system activity review to prevent, detect, contain, and correct security violations.

Assigned Security Responsibility

Designating a Security Officer responsible for developing and implementing security policies and procedures across the covered entity.

Workforce Security

Authorization, supervision, clearance procedures, and termination processes to ensure only authorized personnel access ePHI systems.

Information Access Management

Role-based access controls, access authorization processes, and establishment and modification of access rights for all ePHI systems.

Security Awareness & Training

Ongoing security training for all workforce members including protection against malware, monitoring login attempts, and password management procedures.

Security Incident Procedures

Documented procedures for identifying, reporting, and responding to suspected or known security incidents, including tracking outcomes.

Contingency Planning

Data backup, disaster recovery, emergency mode operation, testing, and revision plans ensuring ePHI availability during emergency situations.

Evaluation

Periodic technical and non-technical evaluation of security policies and procedures in response to environmental or operational changes.

Business Associate Contracts

Written contracts with all business associates ensuring they appropriately safeguard ePHI and comply with applicable HIPAA requirements.

🏢

Physical Safeguards

4 Standards

Facility Access Controls

Contingency operations, facility security plans, access control and validation procedures, and maintenance records for physical access to ePHI systems.

Workstation Use

Policies and procedures specifying proper functions of and physical attributes of the surroundings of workstations that access ePHI.

Workstation Security

Physical safeguards for workstations that access ePHI to restrict access to authorized users only, including screen locks and cable locks.

Device & Media Controls

Disposal of hardware and electronic media containing ePHI, media re-use procedures, accountability tracking, and data backup and storage requirements.

💻

Technical Safeguards

5 Standards

Access Controls

Unique user identification, emergency access procedures, automatic logoff, and encryption/decryption mechanisms for all systems accessing ePHI.

Audit Controls

Hardware, software, and procedural mechanisms to record and examine activity in systems containing or using ePHI — audit logs, access records, and anomaly detection.

Integrity Controls

Policies and procedures ensuring ePHI is not improperly altered or destroyed, including electronic error-checking mechanisms and data integrity validation.

Person Authentication

Procedures to verify that a person seeking access to ePHI is who they claim to be — multi-factor authentication, biometrics, and identity verification protocols.

Transmission Security

Encryption of ePHI in transit, integrity controls preventing improper modification during transmission, and secure protocols for all data exchange.

AI Governance Strategy — Full Detail

Our Framework

Six Core Principles of Healthcare AI Governance

AuditMD's AI governance assessment is structured around six principles that align with international standards (NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001) and U.S. healthcare regulatory expectations.

01 · Transparency

Stakeholders — clinical, administrative, and patient — must be able to understand what an AI system does, what data it uses, and how its outputs influence decisions.

02 · Accountability

Every AI system in clinical or operational use must have a named responsible party — an individual or team accountable for its performance, monitoring, and incident response.

03 · Fairness & Equity

AI outputs must be assessed across patient subgroups defined by race, ethnicity, age, gender, and socioeconomic status. Performance disparities must be documented and addressed.

04 · Safety & Reliability

AI systems must perform within clinically acceptable tolerances under real-world operating conditions, including edge cases and data drift — not only controlled validation environments.

05 · Privacy by Design

AI system architecture must minimize PHI exposure. Data minimization, de-identification, access controls, and audit logging must be built in, not retrofitted post-deployment.

06 · Human Oversight

Clinical AI must preserve meaningful clinician authority over final decisions. Automation bias — the over-reliance on AI outputs at the expense of clinical judgment — must be actively monitored and countered.

Regulatory Landscape

Key Regulatory Frameworks Governing Healthcare AI

AuditMD maps your AI governance posture against the regulatory and standards frameworks that currently govern — or are actively shaping — clinical AI oversight.

Framework / Regulation Issuing Body Scope & Relevance
NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) NIST Voluntary framework for identifying, assessing, and managing AI risks across four functions: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage. Increasingly referenced in regulatory and procurement contexts.
21st Century Cures Act — Information Blocking & Algorithm Transparency ONC / HHS Prohibits practices that restrict access to, exchange, or use of electronic health information. Requires transparency around clinical decision support tools meeting the CDS definition.
FDA Predetermined Change Control Plans FDA Regulates AI/ML-based Software as a Medical Device (SaMD). Requires manufacturers to pre-specify expected algorithm changes — critical for organizations using FDA-regulated clinical AI tools.
Executive Order on Safe, Secure & Trustworthy AI White House / HHS Directs HHS to develop an AI safety program for healthcare. Establishes standards for AI transparency, safety testing, and bias evaluation in health and human services contexts.
ISO/IEC 42001 — AI Management Systems ISO / IEC International standard for establishing, implementing, and maintaining an AI management system. Provides certifiable governance structure increasingly required in global healthcare procurement.
CMS Innovation Center AI Guidance CMS Guidance for value-based care programs and alternative payment models using AI-assisted population health tools. Addresses algorithmic bias in quality measure calculations.
State AI Legislation (NY, CO, TX, IL) State Legislatures A growing number of states are enacting AI-specific legislation covering algorithmic impact assessments, automated decision-making disclosures, and bias testing requirements in high-risk contexts.

DFEAL applies a layered security architecture to all client data, consistent with the HIPAA Security Rule standards we help our clients implement.

All data submitted through auditmd.xyz is transmitted over TLS 1.2 or higher. Our website forms route through a server-side proxy (Netlify serverless function) — webhook URLs and API keys are never exposed to the browser or included in client-side code.

5.2 Storage and Access Control

Client data is stored in Google Workspace (Google Drive and Google Sheets) under the support@auditmd.xyz Google Workspace account. Access controls are role-based: only DFEAL staff with a direct engagement role have access to client-specific folders. External sharing of client folders is disabled.

5.3 Workflow Automation Security

Our engagement automation platform (Zapier) processes form submission data to route it to the correct internal workflow. DFEAL uses Zapier's premium business plan with Business Associate Agreement coverage. Zapier task logs are reviewed monthly and purged on a quarterly schedule.

5.4 Credential and Key Management

API keys, webhook URLs, and credentials used in our platform are stored exclusively as environment variables in Netlify's server-side environment. They are never hardcoded in client-side source code, committed to version control repositories, or transmitted in browser-accessible network requests.

5.5 Multi-Factor Authentication

Multi-factor authentication is enforced on all DFEAL accounts with access to client data, including support@auditmd.xyz (Google Workspace), Zapier, Netlify, and our project management tools.

5.6 Incident Response

In the event of a confirmed data security incident affecting client organisational data, DFEAL will notify affected clients within 72 hours of discovery, provide a written incident summary, and implement remediation measures. Incidents involving potential PHI exposure (if any were inadvertently collected) will be handled per HIPAA Breach Notification Rule requirements.
